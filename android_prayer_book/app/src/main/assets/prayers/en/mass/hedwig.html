June 8
Saint Hedwig, Queen

Optional Memorial

Hedwig, born in the year 1374, was the daughter of King Louis, the king of Hungary and Poland. From her father she inherited the Polish Throne and was crowned in 1384. Marrying the Grand Duke of Lithuania, Władysław Jagiełło, she contributed to the conversion of Lithuania to Christianity. She supported the creation of the Krakow Theological Institute as well as the renewal of the university. Her entire life, she was marked by generosity to the poor. She died in Krakow on July 17, 1399.

From the Common of Holy Men and Women: For Holy Women.

Collect

O God, who gave to Saint Hedwig
the gift of love and contemplation of Christ crucified,
so that she might wisely serve him with love for her brothers,
grant, also to us, that through her intercession
we may love and serve you with unwearied love in prayer and in works.
Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son,
who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit,
one God, for ever and ever.
