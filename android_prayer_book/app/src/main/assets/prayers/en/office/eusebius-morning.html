Morning Prayer

Hymn
O Blessed Eusebius, our holy joy,
To you we raise our voices;
With song overflowing our humble hearts,
And praise you to the highest heaven.
Deign to impassion one and all
United with you in prayer,
That we be able to change for the better,
The adversity of our beloved Homeland.
When sorrows depress our poor spirit,
May your fervent prayers tendered to God
Allow all hearts to be lifted up too,
And banish forever inner turmoil.
The sunrise enlightened the skies,
Scattered the gloomy night's darkness;
Heaven has harkened to your pleas,
Thus filling your soul with its light.
With enlivened hearts we submit our needs,
Not for vain treasures of earth;
But preserved our young from all taint of sin,
Blossoming instead with your holy virtues.
Feel so inclined to enkindle our souls
United with you in prayer,
That we be able to change for the better
The disorder of our beloved Homeland.
Amen.

his práise in the assémbly of the fáithful.

let Síon’s sons exúlt in their kíng.

Bléssed be the Lórd, the Gód of Ísrael! *
He has vísited his péople and redéemed them.
He has raised úp for ús a mighty sáviour *
in the hóuse of Dávid his sérvant,
as he prómised by the líps of holy mén, *
thóse who were his próphets from of óld.
A sáviour who would frée us from our fóes, *
from the hánds of áll who háte us.
So his lóve for our fáthers is fulfílled *
and his hóly covenant remémbered.
He swóre to Ábraham our fáther to gránt us, *
that frée from féar, and sáved from the hánds of our fóes,
we might sérve him in hóliness and jústice *
all the dáys of our lífe in his présence.
As for yóu, little chíld, *
you shall be cálled a próphet of Gód, the Most Hígh.
You shall go ahéad of the Lórd *
to prépare his wáys befóre him,
To make knówn to his péople their salvátion *
through forgíveness of áll their síns,
the loving-kíndness of the héart of our Gód *
who vísits us like the dáwn from on hígh.
He will give líght to those in dárkness, †
those who dwéll in the shádow of déath, *
and gúide us into the wáy of péace.

Intercessions
Let us call on God our Lord, who is merciful and full of compassion, and say with one heart and mind:
R. Let your eyes be open to the prayer of your servants.
Lord, who descended to Moses through a cloud and fire, and spoke with him face to face, just as a man is wont to speak to his friend;
— by the intercession of Blessed Eusebius our Father, take away our sins and free us from evil. R.
God, who, drawing from the Spirit that was in Moses, granted it also to his friends;
— fill us also with the spirit of Blessed Eusebius our Father, who in solitude sought you and the salvation of those nearest him. R.
Lord, who wish that we may seek not the things of earth but those above;
— help us to imitate Blessed Eusebius our Father, so that we may die to sin and the world, and our life may be hidden in Christ with you. R.
Lord, who are full of pity and merciful, patient and deeply compassionate;
— receive our prayers and acts of repentance which we offer you following the example of Blessed Eusebius our Father. R.
Lord, who taught that we should always pray, contemplate the things of heaven and discover the love you bear us;
— grant that we may see you face to face in heaven in communion with our fathers Saint Paul, our First Hermit, and Blessed Eusebius and all the saints of the Pauline Order. R.

Concluding Prayer
O God, who called Blessed Eusebius
into the wilderness of Pilis out of his love for you,
that there he might offer his life in propitiation for the people
and gather the scattered hermits,
grant, we beseech you, that through his life and intercession
our order may be made one in faith and love.
Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son,
who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit,
one God, for ever and ever.
