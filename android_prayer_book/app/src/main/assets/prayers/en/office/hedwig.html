June 8
Saint Hedwig, Queen

Optional Memorial

Hedwig, born in the year 1374, was the daughter of King Louis, the king of Hungary and Poland. From her father she inherited the Polish Throne and was crowned in 1384. Marrying the Grand Duke of Lithuania, Władysław Jagiełło, she contributed to the conversion of Lithuania to Christianity. She supported the creation of the Krakow Theological Institute as well as the renewal of the university. Her entire life, she was marked by generosity to the poor. She died in Krakow on July 17, 1399.

The Virtues of Hedwig were preeminent

Elizabeth, Queen of Hungary, widow of Ludwig, intended to fulfil her frequent promises about assigning her daughter Hedwig to the throne of Poland, for she realised that delaying the hopes of the Poles any longer was not possible without causing a crisis. She brought Hedwig over herself in a mighty company of nobles and soldiers and especially Demetrius, named after four canonised saints, Cardinal Priest and Archbishop of Esztergom, and John, Bishop of the Khanate. She brought with her an outstanding collection of royal furnishings, comprising gold, silver, vessels and costly robes, jewels and cauldrons. At her arrival, about which they had by now practically despaired, the prelates and nobles of Poland competed excitedly to meet her. Their hearts bursting with eagerness, they brought her to Krakow. Processions of all the churches and persons of every rank and standing poured forth to meet her.

So great was the affection of the prelates and nobles towards her, so boundless their love, that, forgetting they were men, they did not consider it demeaning to obey such a remarkable and virtuous woman. Moreover, overcome by their love and affection, they neither granted nor procured a spouse for her, as if she herself was up to ruling the Kingdom of Poland on her own, without a husband.

In Krakow Cathedral, on Sunday, the fifteenth day of October, when the feast of Saint Hedwig is celebrated by Poles, before Bodzenta, Archbishop of Gniezno, Bishops John of Krakow. John of Włocławek and Dobrogost of Poznan, and in the presence of the aforementioned Cardinal Archbishop of Esztergom, the nobles and the military of each Kingdom, namely Poland and Hungary, in a massive crowd with a royal crown they had her installed and consecrated Queen of Poland, endowed with plenary power to administer the Kingdom of Poland until such time as a husband was sought and provided for her.

No wonder, for they knew that, from a tender age, she was so trained and reared that her beauty was surpassed by honour, her appearance by modesty, her comeliness by virtues, her renown and riches by restraint and moderation, her power by gentleness. They saw that, as an uncommonly distinguished person, her charm had been a gift of nature. She was moreover learned in literature, grounded in moral ways, pleasing not so much for her origin as for her womanly dignity, restrained by her nature and outstanding for her natural modesty. By a gift from heaven she had acquired a greater and more attractive beauty to which no age could object: chastity, a unique honour and adornment for married women. It was considered that all manner of virtues were instilled in her right from the cradle, along with the nourishment of milk. After leaving the years of childhood, she grew in wisdom in such a mature and responsible way that all her words and actions seemed to flow from the seriousness of a woman advanced in years.

The concluding prayer as at Morning Prayer.

Morning Prayer

Benedictus ant. Today rejoice, O Saint Queen Hedwig, in the name of all the nations of which you are the mother of the faith.

Concluding Prayer
O God, who gave to Saint Hedwig
the gift of love and contemplation of Christ crucified,
so that she might wisely serve him with love for her brothers,
grant, also to us, that through her intercession
we may love and serve you with unwearied love in prayer and in works.
Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son,
who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit,
one God, for ever and ever.

Evening Prayer

Magnificat ant. We praise you, O Saint Queen Hedwig, who in your life showed love is the highest of all virtues.
