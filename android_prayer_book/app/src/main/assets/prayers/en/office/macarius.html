January 19
St. Macarius the Great, Abbot

Optional Memorial

Macarius was born in Alexandria circa 300 AD. In his youth, he went to the desert near Thebes, where he spent 29 years. Next, he moved to Scetic Desert, where he gave himself to even more rigorously penitential life. He was then ordained a priest. Living in prayer and fasting he received the gift of prophecy as well the ability to work miracles.

From the Common of Pastors, except for the following.

The Office of Readings

Second Reading
A reading from
Conferences of the Fathers by Saint John Cassian, priest and abbot.
(Liber II, Conlatio XV, 3)
Of a dead man raised to life by Abbot Macarius.

As also we remember that a dead man was raised to life by Abbot Macarius who was the first to find a home in the desert of Scete. For when a certain heretic who followed the error of Eunomius was trying by dialectic subtlety to destroy the simplicity of the Catholic faith, and had already deceived a large number of men, the blessed Macarius was asked by some Catholics, who were terribly disturbed by the horror of such an upset, to set free the simple folk of all Egypt from the peril of infidelity, and came for this purpose. And when the heretic had approached him with his dialectic art, and wanted to drag him away in his ignorance to the thorns of Aristotle, the blessed Macarius put a stop to his chatter with apostolic brevity, saying: “the kingdom of God is not in word but in power.” (1 Corinthians 4:20) Let us go therefore to the tombs, and let us invoke the name of the Lord over the first dead man we find, and let us, as it is written, “show our faith by our works,” (James 2:14) that by His testimony the manifest proofs of a right faith may be shown, and we may prove the clear truth not by an empty discussion of words but by the power of miracles and that judgment which cannot be deceived. And when he heard this the heretic … fled away, and at once escaped out of all Egypt.

When then the blessed Macarius had taken up his position by a most ancient corpse, he said O man, if that heretic and son of perdition had come hither with me, and, while he was standing by, I had exclaimed and invoked the name of Christ my God, say in the presence of these who were almost perverted by his fraud, whether you would have arisen. Then he arose and replied with words of assent. And then Abbot Macarius asked him what he had formerly been when he enjoyed life here, or in what age of men he had lived, or if he had then known the name of Christ, and he replied that he had lived under kings of most ancient date, and declared that in those days he had never heard the name of Christ. To whom once more Abbot Macarius: Sleep, said he, in peace with the others in your own order, to be roused again by Christ in the end. All this power then and grace of his which was in him would perhaps have always been hidden, unless the needs of the whole province which was endangered, and his entire devotion to Christ, and unfeigned love, had forced him to perform this miracle.

Cf. Mt 22:37; Jas 2:17-18
Responsory

R. You must love the Lord your God will all your heart, with all your soul and with all your mind. * Show me your faith apart from your works, and I by my works will show you my faith.
V. Faith by itself, if it has no works is dead. * Show me your faith apart from your works, and I by my works will show you my faith.

The concluding prayer as at Morning Prayer.

Morning Prayer

Benedictus ant. You have obtained blessing and salvation from God, you who have always sought the Lord.

Concluding Prayer
O God, almighty Father, grant we beseech you,
that we may imitate of the life of blessed Macarius the Abbot,
that we may be made worthy of your highest love
and may receive with him a like reward
from the everlasting spring of our Saviour Jesus Christ.
Who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit,
one God, for ever and ever.

Evening Prayer

Magnificat ant. The Lord looks on those who revere him, on those who hope in his love.
