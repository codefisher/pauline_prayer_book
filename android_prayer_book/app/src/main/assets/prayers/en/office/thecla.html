June 8
St Thecla, Virgin and Martyr

Optional Memorial

Tecla is thought to have come from Iconium. She was said to have received Baptism from St Paul the Apostle during his first journey through Asia Minor (the years 45-49). She made a vow of Chasity and remained unmarried. She accompanied St Paul in his journeys, ministering to him. She is supposedly to have lived long life, well into her 80s, of which she spent a significant amount as a hermit.

From the Common of Martyrs: For a Virgin Martyr, except for the following.

Second Reading
From
Concerning Virgins by Saint Ambrose of Milan, Bishop
(De virginibus II, 19-21)
The Blessed Virgin Mary teaches us how to live, and Saint Thecla how we must depart from life

Let, then, holy Mary instruct you in the discipline of life, and Thecla teach you how to be offered, for she, avoiding nuptial intercourse, and condemned through her husband's rage, changed even the disposition of wild beasts by their reverence for virginity. For being made ready for the wild beasts, when avoiding the gaze of men, she offered her vital parts to a fierce lion, caused those who had turned away their immodest looks to turn them back modestly.

The beast was to be seen lying on the ground, licking her feet, showing without a sound that it could not injure the sacred body of the virgin. So the beast reverenced his prey, and forgetful of his own nature, put on that nature which men had lost. One could see, as it were, by some transfusion of nature, men clothed with savageness, goading the beast to cruelty, and the beast kissing the feet of the virgin, teaching them what was due from men. Virginity has in itself so much that is admirable, that even lions admire it. Food did not induce them though kept without their meal; no impulse hurried them on when excited; anger did not exasperate them when stirred up, nor did their habits lead them blindly as they were wont, nor their own natural disposition possess them with fierceness. They set an example of piety when reverencing the martyr; and gave a lesson in favor of chastity when they did nothing but kiss the virgin's feet, with their eyes turned to the ground, as though through modesty, fearing that any male, even a beast, should see the virgin naked.

Some one will say: “Why have you brought forward the example of Mary, as if any one could be found to imitate the Lord's mother? And why that of Thecla, whom the Apostle of the Gentiles trained? Give us a teacher of our own sort if you wish for disciples.” I will, therefore, set before you a recent example of this sort, that you may understand that the Apostle is the teacher, not of one only, but of all.

Responsory

R. Your souls, O virgins, are an altar to God, * on which Christ daily offers himself for the redemption of the body.
V. O blessed virgins, you are gardens full of flowers, temples of devotion, the altars of the priest, * on which Christ daily offers himself for the redemption of the body.

The concluding prayer as at Morning Prayer.

Morning Prayer

Benedictus ant. Hail, O brave virgin, a pure victim offered in sacrifice to God, who followed the crucified lamb for us.

Concluding Prayer
O God, for the glory of your name blessed Thecla, virgin and martyr,
withstood the great contest of sufferings
with her faith unconquered,
grant to us in imitation of the her faith
to despise the success of the world
and to tremble at none of its adversity.
Through our Lord Jesus Christ your Son,
who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit,
one God, for ever and ever.

Evening Prayer

Magnificat ant. One sacrifice, one victory: the virgin remained and merited the plam of martyrdom.
