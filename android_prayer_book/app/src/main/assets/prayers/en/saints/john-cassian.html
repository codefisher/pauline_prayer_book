February 25
Saint John Cassian, Abbot

Memorial

John Cassian was born around AD 360, most likely in modern Romania. When he was twenty, he came to Palestine and became a monk in one of the monasteries in Bethlehem. Next, he spent ten years in Egypt among the monks there, where he came to know renowned masters of the eremitical life. Then, in the years following, he lived in Rome. There he received priestly orders and befriended the future Pope Leo the Great. Around 415, he founded two monasteries, one for monks (Saint Victor’s) and another for virgins (The Saviour’s).
