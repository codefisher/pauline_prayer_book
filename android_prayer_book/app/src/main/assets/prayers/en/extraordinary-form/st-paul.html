10 January Our Patriarch Saint
Paul the First Hermit
Double I Class with Common octave

Ps. 54:8-9
Introit

So I would escape far away and take refuge in the desert, I would hasten to find a shelter from the raging wind, from the destructive storm. (T.P. Allelúja, allelúja.) Ps. Ibid 1 O God, listen to my prayer, and do not hide from my pleading, attend to me and reply. V. Glory be.

For the brothers of our Order

Collect

O God, who blessed Paul our Father, having separated from the pomp of the world, to flee to the desert, and there in contemplation of your name you found worthy to accomplish the course of life; grant to us, by the merit of his intercession; that we might both not regard the mundane and to merit to strive after heaven. Through our Lord.

Collect
For Others

All powerful and merciful God, who blessed Paul the first Hermit, your Confessor, to be dead to the world, and to cause to live wonderfully in unity with you; grant, we pray, that by his intercession, your faithful might not regard earthly things, and they strive after you by a singular devotion of the mind. Through our Lord.

And if it is the Common Octave of the Epiphany

Collect

O God who today your only begotten Son your revealed to the gentiles by a guiding star: graciously grant; that we who now know you by faith and might be lead to contemplate the sight of your majesty. Through our Lord.

Reading from the Book of Wisdom
Wis. 10:10-13

When a righteous man fled from his brother’s wrath, she guided him on straight paths; she showed him the kingdom of God, and gave him knowledge of angels;[e] she prospered him in his labors, and increased the fruit of his toil. When his oppressors were covetous, she stood by him and made him rich. She protected him from his enemies, and kept him safe from those who lay in wait for him; in his arduous contest she gave him the victory, so that he might learn that godliness is more powerful than anything. When a righteous man was sold, wisdom[f] did not desert him, but delivered him from sin. She descended with him into the dungeon,

Offertory Hosea 2:14 I will bring her into the wilderness, and speak tenderly to her. (T.P. allelúja.)

Secret

Accept these gifts, we pray, Lord, which we offer to you calling to mind the birth of blessed Paul your confessor, both for our salvation and your divine majesty. Through Christ our Lord.

For the Octave of the Epiphany

Secret

Graciously look down, we beseech you Lord, upon the gifts of your Church: by which now not gold, frankincense, and myrrh are offered; but the same gifts Jesus Chirst, your Son, our lord, declared, immolated and assumed: Who lives and reigns with you.

Preface and Communicántes of the Epiphany; after the Octave the common Preface.

Communion Antiphon Ps. 110:4 et 5 He makes us remember his wonders. The Lord is compassion and love. He gives food to those who fear him. (T. P. Allelúja.)

Prayer after Communion

We humbly beseech you, Lord: that as blessed Paul was restored by temporal nourishment, and you willed to come to you, thus even now we having been satisfied by these sacred gifts, the fellowship of your glory we might come to enjoy fully forever. Through Christ our Lord.

For the Octave of the Epiphany

Prayer after Communion

Grant, we beseech you, all powerful God; that, we who solemnly celebrate the service, might strive to come to know a purity of mine. Through Christ our Lord.
