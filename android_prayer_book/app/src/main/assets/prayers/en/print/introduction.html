Introduction

United with the Universal Church, we remain in the time of preparation for the Great Jubilee of the Year 2000. In this prepara- tion, we feel a need of personal conversion and renewal of spirit. This desire becomes for us a unique invitation in this current year of 1999 which is called in the Church the “Year of God the Fa- ther.”

The yearning for our religious perfection is deeply linked with love. Our Holy Father, Pope John Paul II, in speaking to us Paulines, on the occasion of the 600th anniversary of Jasna Gora, called us to be the “messengers of love, the servants of love.” In order to achieve this, the Pope said: “to express that love, to serve that love, you must continually strive to be filled with love.” We attain that love only through prayer. In our Constitutions we read, “It is in prayer that we are able to recognise the plan of God for us and for the work. We are able likewise to enter more deeply into the salvific mystery of Christ, thereby becoming joined with the Father in the Holy Spirit in the work of continually renewing the face of the earth. By this means we assist in the building up and propagation of the Kingdom of God on earth.” (art.26) Mary, the Mother of our Order, and St. Paul the First Her- mit, our Patriarch, continue to encourage us in the contemplation of God and perseverance in prayer.

I feel highly honoured that thanks to the dedicated labour of my Confreres, this Pauline Prayer Book can be published. I trust, that through this new edition, our whole Religious Community will glorify God the Father with one Spirit and one heart.

Fr. Stanislaw Turek, OSPPE
General Superior of the Order of St. Paul the First Hermit
