June 8
St Thecla, Virgin and Martyr

Optional Memorial

Tecla is thought to have come from Iconium. She was said to have received Baptism from St Paul the Apostle during his first journey through Asia Minor (the years 45-49). She made a vow of Chasity and remained unmarried. She accompanied St Paul in his journeys, ministering to him. She is supposedly to have lived long life, well into her 80s, of which she spent a significant amount as a hermit.

From the Common of Martyrs: For a Virgin Martyr, or from the Common of Virgins: For One Virgin

Collect

O God, for the glory of your name blessed Thecla, virgin and martyr, withstood the great contest of sufferings with her faith unconquered, grant to us in imitation of the her faith to despise the success of the world and to tremble at none of its adversity. Through our Lord Jesus Christ your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
