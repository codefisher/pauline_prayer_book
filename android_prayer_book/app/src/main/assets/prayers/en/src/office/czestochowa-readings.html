A reading from the letter of St. Paul to the Galatians

Scripture represents us all as under the bondage of sin; it was faith in Jesus Christ that was to impart the promised blessing to all those who believe in him. Until faith came, we were all being kept in bondage to the law, waiting for the faith that was one day to be revealed. So that the law was our tutor, bringing us to Christ, to find in faith our justification. When faith comes, then we are no longer under the rule of a tutor; through faith in Christ Jesus you are all now God’s sons. All you who have been baptised in Christ’s name have put on the person of Christ; no more Jew or Gentile, no more slave and freeman, no more male or female; you are all one person in Jesus Christ. And if you belong to Christ, then you are indeed Abraham’s children; the promised inheritance is yours.

Consider this; one who comes into his property while he is still a child has no more liberty than one of the servants, though all the estate is his; he is under the control of guardians and trustees, until he reaches the age prescribed by his father. So it was with us; in those childish days of ours we toiled away at the schoolroom tasks which the world gave us, till the appointed time came. Then God sent out his Son on a mission to us. He took birth from a woman, took birth as a subject of the law, so as to ransom those who were subject to the law, and make us sons by adoption. To prove that you are sons, God sent out the Spirit of his Son into your hearts, crying out in us, Abba, Father. No longer, then, are you a slave, you are a son; and because you are a son, you have, by divine appointment, the son’s right of inheritance.

R/ Behold, the appointed time is here: God has sent his own Son into the world, born of a woman, born under the law, * to redeem those under the law.
V/ For the great love he bore us, God sent his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh * to redeem those under the law.

From the Homily of St. John Paul II, for the 600th Anniversary of the Shrine of Jasna Gora

The liturgy above all, distinctly presents the wondrous mystery of the Jasna Gora Shrine, as we read the Johannine Gospel account of the wedding feast at Cana. In this Gospel passage we are told of the presence of the Mother of Jesus: “the Mother of Jesus was there,” and of the fact that Jesus himself and his disciples were invited. The event takes place at the beginning of the Son of Mary’s ministry of teaching, at the beginning of his overall public ministry in Galilee.

This particular Gospel event brings to mind the millennial anniversary of the so-called Baptism of Poland. Through this event in 966, through the “Baptism”, at the start of our ministry as a nation, Jesus Christ was invited to the Fatherland, as if to a Polish Cana. At the same time, invited together with him, came Mary. She came and was present together with her Son, as is demonstrated by numerous testimonies gathered from the initial first centuries of Christianity in Poland, most particularly in the ancient hymn, “God-bearer.” In Cana, Mary says to the stewards of the feast: “Do whatever he tells you.” Beginning from 1382, Mary stands before the sons and daughters of this land, before whole generations, and repeats the same words. Jasna Gora has become a particular place of evangelisation on our journey. The message of the Good News obtains a unique expressiveness here, and simultaneously becomes mediated by Mary as it were. Jasna Gora has brought this motherly trait to Polish Christianity and the life of the Church in this land, the beginnings of which were closely connected with the events of Cana. Do whatever he tells you. And what does Christ tell us? Is it not first of all that which we find so tersely expressed in the letter of Saint Paul to the Galatians? But when the fullness of time had come, God sent his Son, born of a woman, born under the law, to redeem them that were under the law, that we might receive the adoption of sons. And because you are sons, God has sent the Spirit of his Son into our hearts, crying, Abba, Father. So through God you are no longer a slave, but a son; and if a son, then an heir.

Christ tells us this from one generation to the next. He speaks through all that he does and teaches. He speaks especially through he who is. He is the Son of God - and he comes to bestow upon us adopted sonship. Having received in the power of the Holy Spirit the dignity of the sons of God, in the power of this same Spirit, we say to God: “Father.” As sons of God we cannot be slaves. Our sonship of God carries within itself an inheritance of freedom.

Christ present together with his Mother in the Polish Cana places before us, from generation to generation, the great ideal of freedom. Freedom is given to man by God as a measure of his dignity. At the same time it is entrusted to him. “Freedom is not a relief from hardship, it is the hardship of greatness” says the poet. Man can use freedom well, or abuse it. He can build up through it, or destroy. The message of Jasna Gora carries with it an invitation to the inheritance of the sons of God. A call to a life of freedom. A summons to use freedom well, to build up and not destroy.

This is the evangelical call of Jasna Gora to a life of freedom worthy of the sons of her Son. This same thing happens in Jasna Gora. How many have passed through Jasna Gora these past six centuries? How many have converted here, passing from an evil to a wholesome use of their freedom? How many have regained the true dignity of the adopted sons of God? How much could the Icon of the Black Madonna relate to us all of this? How much could the confessionals of the whole Basilica tell us? How many things could the Way of the Cross on the rampart of the Shrine reveal? An enormous chapter in the history of human souls. This is perhaps the most fundamental dimension of the sixth centenary of Jasna Gora. It has remained and will remain within living people, sons and daughters of this land, when God sends into their hearts his Holy Spirit in such a way that in all interior truth they can call out: “Abba, Father!”

We wish to continue to, in accordance with the word’s of today’s Gospel, invite Jesus into the life of our Fatherland, in the same way he was invited, together with his Mother, to Cana. We say to Christ through Mary: Be with us at every moment! We make this invitation here, at Jasna Gora.

Once again we embrace with our gaze and heart our Shrine of Jasna Gora. We listen intently to the words of the psalm in today’s liturgy: “Go about Sion, count the number of its towers… so that you may tell the next generation, that God is our God for ever.”

V/ Go through Sion, count the number of its towers, that you may tell future generations, that God is our God forever * His holy mountain, beautiful in elevation, is the joy of all the earth.

Te Deum We praise you, O God: * we acclaim you as the Lord. - Everlasting Father, * - all the world bows down before you. All the angels sing your praise, * the hosts of heaven and all the angelic powers, - all the cherubim and seraphim * - call out to you in unending song: -- Holy, Holy, Holy, * -- is the Lord God of angel hosts! The heavens and the earth are filled * with your majesty and glory. - The glorious band of apostles, * - the noble company of prophets, the white-robed army who shed their blood for Christ, * all sing your praise. - And to the ends of the earth * - your holy Church proclaims her faith in you: -- Father, whose majesty is boundless, + -- your true and only Son, who is to be adored, * -- the Holy Spirit sent to be our Advocate. You, Christ, are the king of glory, * Son of the eternal Father. - When you took our nature to save mankind * - you did not shrink from birth in the Virgin’s womb. You overcame the power of death * opening the Father’s kingdom to all who believe in you. - Enthroned at God’s right hand in the glory of the Father, * - you will come in judgement according to your promise. You redeemed your people by your precious blood. * Come, we implore you, to our aid. - Grant us with the saints * - a place in eternal glory. The final part of the hymn may be omitted: Lord, save your people * and bless your inheritance. - Rule them and uphold them * - for ever and ever. Day by day we praise you: * we acclaim you now and to all eternity. - In your goodness, Lord, keep us free from sin. * - Have mercy on us, Lord, have mercy. May your mercy always be with us, Lord, * for we have hoped in you. - In you, Lord, we put our trust: * - we shall not be put to shame. O Almighty and Merciful God, you have wondrously established the most Blessed Virgin Mary to be the perpetual help and defence of the Christian people, and made her holy image at Jasna Gora renowned by the solemn veneration of the faithful. Graciously grant that defended by her protection as we fight the battle of life, may we be victorious over the evil spirits at the hour of our death. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, Your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God for ever and ever.
