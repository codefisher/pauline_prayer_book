Morning Prayer

Giver of life, eternal Lord, Thy own redeemed defend; Mother of grace, thy children save, and help them to the end. Ye thousand thousand Angel hosts, assist us in our need; Ye Patriarchs, with the Prophet choir, for our forgiveness plead. Forerunner blest, and Thou who still dost heaven's dread keys retain; Ye glorious Apostles all, unloose our guilty chain. Army of Martyrs, holy Priests, in beautiful array; Ye happy troops of Virgins chaste, wash all our stains away. All ye who high above the stars in heavenly glory reign, May we through your prevailing prayers unto your joys attain. Praise, honor, to the Father be, praise to His only Son; Praise, Holy Paraclete, to Thee, while endless ages run.

Ant. 3: This is the glory of all the saints, who followed the footsteps of Christ.

Psalm 149

Síng a new sóng to the Lórd, * - his práise in the assémbly of the fáithful. Let Ísrael rejóice in its Máker, * - let Síon’s sons exúlt in their kíng. Let them práise his náme with dáncing * - and make músic with tímbrel and hárp. For the Lórd takes delíght in his péople. * - He crówns the póor with salvátion. Let the fáithful rejóice in their glóry, * - shout for jóy and táke their rést. Let the práise of Gód be on their líps * - and a twó-edged swórd in their hánd, to déal out véngeance to the nátions * - and púnishment on áll the péoples; to bínd their kíngs in cháins * - and their nóbles in fétters of íron; to cárry out the séntence pre-ordáined: * - this hónour is for áll his fáithful.

God, font of sanctity, who causes to shine your manifold graces wonderfully in the saints; — grant to us to celebrate in this, your greatness. R/ Most provident and eternal God, who shows to us the image of your Son in the saints; — grant, that we might come to union with Christ through their efforts. R/ King of heaven, who through faithful followers of Christ hasten the coming of the future city; — grant that we might learn from them down the safer road that leads to it. R/ God, who in the sacrifice of the body of your Son join us to those in heaven; — increase our devotion, that we might be conformed to their perfect worship. R/

Our Father…

Almighty ever-living God, who ever adorn, protect and keep your Church with the various splendours of the Saints, graciously grant that, by the prayers of Blessed Paul the First Hermit and his holy sons and daughters, the merits of all of whom you have granted us to honour on one feast day, both that we may be purified from every fault here, and that we may fully enjoy eternal glory in the future. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
