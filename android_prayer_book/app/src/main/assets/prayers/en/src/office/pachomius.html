May 9
St Pachomius, Abbot

Memorial

Pachomius was an Egyptian Monk, the writer of the first rule of religious life and of coenobitism. He was born around 287AD in Egypt. In his youth he was recruited into the Roman Army. During his service, under the influence of the selfless help of Christians, he converted to the faith. He resolved to give himself to the exclusive service of God. He went into the Desert, where he lived in austere asceticism under the guidance of St Palemon, following which he lived a life of solitude in Tabenna, where he himself founded a monastic community.

From the Common of Men Saints, except for the following.

The Office of Readings

From the Rule and from the Councils of St. Pachomius, Abbot

If anyone approaches the door of the monastery wanting to renounce the world and be included in the number of the brothers, he shall not be free to enter but shall be first be reported to the Father of the monastery, and he shall remain for a few days outside in front of the door. He shall be taught the Lord’s prayer and the Psalms, as many as he can learn thoroughly: and he shall diligently submit to a test in case he has perchance committed some wrongdoing and in a troubled state taken off fearfully on the spur of the moment, or is under some kind of control; he must show whether he can renounce his parents and despise his personal possessions.

If they see that he is fit for prayer and everything else, then he shall be taught the remaining monastic discipline, which ones he must observe and carry out, whom he is obliged to obey, whether in the community of all the brothers or in the house to which he is to be assigned, or in the order of eating; so that, equipped and made perfect in every good work, he may be joined with the brothers. Then they shall strip him of his secular clothes and clothe him in the habit of the monks.

We must remain firm and unmoving so that we may curb wandering thoughts, which boil over like water, by constantly meditating on God’s law, by which, tearing down the law of carnal concupiscence, we may be able to seek all that is pleasing to God and be kept safe from the world’s troubles and all pride, which is the worst kind of madness, worse than all other evils. Holding our Lord always before our eyes, let us recall the suffering of the crucifixion and death of him through whom we have been redeemed and brought to life. Let us therefore hate the world and everything in it; let us also hate every bodily comfort. Let us reject this life so that we may be able to live for God.

Let us therefore, beloved, be mindful of our profession, how we promised to be devoted to God, for this is the very thing that will be asked of us on the day of judgment. Let us hunger, thirst, endure poverty, lack of sleep. Let us, immersed in our psalms and prayers, groan from the depths of our heart, pouring forth our tears. Let us test our true selves through careful examination in the hope that we may, even in some way, deserve to be found worthy of God in virtue of his boundless clemency. Let us not shrink from the distress of tribulation so that we may win rest in the consolation of our Lord and deserve to obtain the repose of an immortal and eternal life.

R/ Blessed God, grant us the favour of fittingly completing the service we have begun that we may be made worthy of your promises, * that you may dwell in our body, in our soul and in our spirit.
V/ That we may always be perfect in loving you, walking in your presence at your good pleasure, * that you may dwell in our body, in our soul and in our spirit.

The concluding prayer as at Morning Prayer.

Morning Prayer

Benedictus ant. The wise man, Saint Pachomius, built his house on rock.

O Lord our God, grant to us, we beseech you, by the intercession of blessed Pachomius who purified himself in the desert for the sake of the monastical and cenobitical life and wrought a holy and bloodless martyrdom, that we may be acceptable to you in words and deeds and succeed in obtaining the inheritance of the kingdom of heaven. Through our Lord Jesus Christ your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.

Evening Prayer

Magnificat ant. Where brothers gather to praise the Lord, there descends his blessing like dew.
