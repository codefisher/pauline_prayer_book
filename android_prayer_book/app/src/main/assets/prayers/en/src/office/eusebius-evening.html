Evening Prayer

Deep in the vast wilderness A flame illuminates the darkness; Bright prayer rises Of saintly Eusebius' hermits. O Eusebius, our Father, Astonished as we are with delight, Favored as you are by God, Gather your people as one. Deep in the vast wilderness Bright flames are deeply hidden. By lives led for the glory of God, And in praise of the Blessed Virgin Mary. O Eusebius, our Father, Astonished as we are with delight, Favored as you are by God, Father your people as one. Deep in our own lowly souls, May flames grow always anew, In praise of the Father and Son, And Holy Spirit forever. O Eusebius, our Father, Astonished as we are with delight, Favored as you are by God, Gather your people as one. Amen.

Psalm 111 (112)

Happy the mán who féars the Lórd, * - who tákes delíght in all his commánds. His sóns will be pówerful on éarth; * - the chíldren of the úpright are bléssed. Ríches and wéalth are in his hóuse; * - his jústice stands fírm for éver. He is a líght in the dárkness for the úpright: * - he is génerous, mérciful and júst. The góod man takes píty and lénds, * - he condúcts his affáirs with hónour. The júst man will néver wáver: * - hé will be remémbered for éver. He has no féar of évil néws; * - with a fírm heart he trústs in the Lórd. With a stéadfast héart he will not féar; * - he will sée the dównfall of his fóes. Open-hánded, he gíves to the póor; + - his jústice stands fírm for éver. * - His héad will be ráised in glóry. The wícked man sées and is ángry, + - grinds his téeth and fádes awáy; * - the desíre of the wícked leads to dóom.

Ant. That you may tell the next generation that such is our God, our God for ever and always. It is he who leads us.

My soul glórifies the Lórd, * - my spirit rejóices in Gód, my Sáviour. He looks on his sérvant in her lówliness; * - henceforth all áges will cáll me bléssed. The Almíghty works márvels for me. * - Hóly his náme! His mércy is from age to áge, * - on thóse who féar him. He púts forth his árm in stréngth * - and scátters the proud-héarted. Ant. Shout for joy, you heavens; exult, you earth! You mountains, break into praises! For the Lord consoles his people and takes pity on those who are afflicted.

God, after whom all fatherhood is named in heaven and on earth, chose Blessed Eusebius of Esztergom to consecrate his life to him in solitude and there with his brother hermits to love God and his neighbour. Let us pray to him with humble heart:
R/ Lord, save your people. Help us to reject everything that is opposed to the name of Christ; — so that we may imitate you with a sincere heart. R/ Accept us as a holy, living sacrifice, pleasing to you; — so that with renewed minds we may together learn what is good and pleasing to you. R/ Grant that we may be clothed with kindness, gentleness and forbearance; — and, supporting one another in turn, we may have charity towards all. R/ Turn our eyes towards the rock from which we have been carved; — so that we may all faithfully pursue our calling according to the example of our fathers Saint Paul, the First Hermit, and Blessed Eusebius. R/ Grant that together with all the Saints of the Pauline Order; — we may contemplate you in heaven without end. R/

Our Father…

O God, who called Blessed Eusebius into the wilderness of Pilis out of his love for you, that there he might offer his life in propitiation for the people and gather the scattered hermits, grant, we beseech you, that through his life and intercession our order may be made one in faith and love. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
