Morning Prayer

We sing to Paul the first hermit, great honour of our Order. The caves, and woods, and deserts filling with the divine presence. You, not fearing the devil’s witchcrafts, you, not minding the rock’s harshness, nor the wilderness of far lands, nor with drawing your resolution. You, whom a black raven nourished and a small spring the thirst quenched; Whom a large palm’s leafage covered, and a couch the soil provided. You, whose mind kept always soaring, far from world contamination. You, who joined the crowds of angels, that are known to God the Mighty. Turn your glance and hear the prayers, blessed Father, Blessed Paul, grant us lives afar from foulness, grant its mantles of the heavens. Be glory to the Father, and to the Son and Spirit, be glory to the Trinity, for ages without end. Amen.

Our Father…

God, you granted that Saint Paul, our Father, achieve sanctity in the desert, grant through his intercession, that we, who are striving to develop in ourselves a spirit of prayer and service, may come ever closer to you in love. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
