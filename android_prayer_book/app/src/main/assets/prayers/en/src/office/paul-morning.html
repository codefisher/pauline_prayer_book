Morning Prayer

We sing to Paul first hermit, Great honour of our Order. The caves, and woods, and deserts Filling with divine presence You, not fearing devil’s witchcrafts, You, not minding the rock’s harshness, Nor the wilderness of far lands, Nor with drawing your resolution. You, whom a black raven nourished And a small spring the thirst quenched Whom a large palm’s leafage covered And a couch the sole provided. You, whose mind kept always soaring, Far from world contamination. You, who joined the crowds of angels, That are known to God the Mighty. Turn your glance and hear the prayers Blessed Father, Blessed Paul, Grant us lives afar from foulness Grant its mantles of the heavens Be glory to Father And to the Son and Spirit Be glory to the Trinity For ages without end. Amen

Ant. 1: O that I had wings like a dove to fly away and be at rest. So I would escape far away and take refuge in the desert. I would hasten to find a shelter from the raging wind.

Psalm 62 (63)

O God, you are my God, for you I long; * - for you my soul is thirsting. My body pines for you * - like a dry, weary land without water. So I gaze on you in the sanctuary * - to see your strength and your glory. For your love is better than life, * - my lips will speak your praise. So I will bless you all my life, * - in your name I will lift up my hands. My soul shall be filled as with a banquet, * - my mouth shall praise you with joy. On my bed I remember you. * - On you I muse through the night for you have been my help; * - in the shadow of your wings I rejoice. My soul clings to you; * - your right hand holds me fast.

Ant. 3: The blessed Hermits rejoice in glory and share in the heavenly banquet.

Psalm 149

Sing a new song to the Lord, * - his praise in the assembly of the faithful. Let Israel rejoice in its Maker, * - let Zion’s sons exult in their king. Let them praise his name with dancing * - and make music with timbrel and harp. For the Lord takes delight in his people. * - He crowns the poor with salvation. Let the faithful rejoice in their glory, * - shout for joy and take their rest. Let the praise of God be on their lips * - and a two-edged sword in their hand, to deal out vengeance to the nations * - and punishment on all the peoples; to bind their kings in chains * - and their nobles in fetters of iron; to carry out the sentence pre-ordained: * - this honour is for all his faithful.

Benedictus ant. Amid the host of angels and the choirs of Prophets and Apostles, Paul ascends into heaven.

Blessed be the Lord, the God of Israel! * - He has visited his people and redeemed them. He has raised up for us a mighty saviour * - in the house of David his servant, as he promised by the lips of holy men, * - those who were his prophets from of old. A saviour who would free us from our foes, * - from the hands of all who hate us. So his love for our fathers is fulfilled * - and his holy covenant remembered. He swore to Abraham our father to grant us, * - that free from fear, and saved from the hands of our foes, we might serve him in holiness and justice * - all the days of our life in his presence. As for you, little child, * - you shall be called a prophet of God, the Most High. You shall go ahead of the Lord * - to prepare his ways before him, To make known to his people their salvation * - through forgiveness of all their sins, the loving-kindness of the heart of our God * - who visits us like the dawn from on high. He will give light to those in darkness, + - those who dwell in the shadow of death, * - and guide us into the way of peace.

Ant. Amid the host of angels and the choirs of Prophets and Apostles, Paul ascends into heaven.

Through the merits of our Saviour, who gave us, the example of a life of prayer, let us ask our Heavenly Father to bestow his Spirit, as a support to our weakness.
R/ May your Spirit intercede for us. Father who desires to be glorified by pure hearts and a life devoted to prayer, — make us temples of your presence. R/ Father, who guides our monastic community along paths of prayer and penance, — renew in us the spirit of the Patriarchs and Founders of our Order. R/ Father, who established for us a place in your Church, — make our communities and houses the tabernacles of community spirit and apostolic service. R/ Father, who has mercifully linked us to Mary, the Mother of Christ, — make us faithful ministers of the Marian mission of our Order. R/

Our Father…

God, you granted that Saint Paul, our Father, achieve sanctity in the desert, grant through his intercession, that we who are striving to develop in ourselves a spirit of prayer and service may come ever closer to you in love. Through our Lord Jesus Christ your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
