Evening Prayer

To Thy poor servants reconciled show mercy, Christ, for whom the mild and Virgin Patroness this grace implore before Thy Father's face. Ye purpled martyrs, you, now dressed in white because your lives confessed your Lord on earth, us exiles call unto the fatherland of all. O choir of virgins, stainless band! and ye for whom the desert-land made sure the way to heavenly rest, prepare us mansions with the blest. The race perfidious expel from regions where the faithful dwell; let one sole shepherd be our guide, all Christians in one fold abide. Glory, O Father! to Thy name; eternal Son, to Thine the same; to Holy Paraclete be praise throughout the everlasting days.

God, the crown of all the saints, rejoicing we pray:
R/ Through the intercession of the saints, save us, Lord. You, who gave strength of spirit to the monks and hermits, that faith might be witnessed even to the fullness of ascetic virtue; — grant, that all christians might become faithful witnesses of your Son. R/ You, who are the abundantly blessed virgins with the sublime gift of the imitation of Christ and his Mother; — grant, that we might love virginity consecrated to you so as to give a sign of the goodness of heaven. R/ You, who in all the saints manifest your face and presence; — grant, that your faithful, through their intercession, might always live with you. R/ You, who are goodness and mercy, accept our departed brothers to the eternal communion with your saints in heaven; — and grant to us, that we might be united eternally with you in your glory. R/

Our Father…

Almighty ever-living God, who ever adorn, protect and keep your Church with the various splendours of the Saints, graciously grant that, by the prayers of Blessed Paul the First Hermit and his holy sons and daughters, the merits of all of whom you have granted us to honour on one feast day, both that we may be purified from every fault here, and that we may fully enjoy eternal glory in the future. Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son, who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God, for ever and ever.
