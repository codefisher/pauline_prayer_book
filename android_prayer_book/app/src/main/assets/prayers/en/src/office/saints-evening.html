Evening Prayer

To Thy poor servants reconciled show mercy, Christ, for whom the mild and Virgin Patroness this grace implore before Thy Father's face. Ye purpled martyrs, you, now dressed in white because your lives confessed your Lord on earth, us exiles call unto the fatherland of all. O choir of virgins, stainless band! and ye for whom the desert-land made sure the way to heavenly rest, prepare us mansions with the blest. The race perfidious expel from regions where the faithful dwell; let one sole shepherd be our guide, all Christians in one fold abide. Glory, O Father! to Thy name; eternal Son, to Thine the same; to Holy Paraclete be praise throughout the everlasting days.

Psalm 115 (116)

I trusted, even when I said: * - ‘I am sorely afflicted,’ and when I said in my alarm: * - ‘No man can be trusted.’ How can I repay the Lord * - for his goodness to me? A thanksgiving sacrifice I make; * - I will call on the Lord’s name. My vows to the Lord I will fulfil * - before all his people, in the courts of the house of the Lord, * - in your midst, O Jerusalem.

My soul glorifies the Lord, * - my spirit rejoices in God, my Saviour. He looks on his servant in her lowliness; * - henceforth all ages will call me blessed. The Almighty works marvels for me. * - Holy his name! His mercy is from age to age, * - on those who fear him. He puts forth his arm in strength * - and scatters the proud hearted. He casts the mighty from their thrones * - and raises the lowly. He fills the starving with good things, * - send the rich away empty. He protects Israel, his servant, * - remembering his mercy, the mercy promised to our fathers, * - to Abraham and his sons forever.

God, the crown of all the saints, rejoicing we pray:
R/ Through the intercession of the saints save us, Lord. You, who gave strength of spirit to the monks and hermits, that faith might be witnessed to even to the fullness of ascetic virtue, — grant, that all christians might become faithful witnesses of your Son. R/ You, who are the abundantly blessed virgins with the sublime gift of the imitation of Christ and his Mother, — grant, that we might love virginity consecrated to you so as to give a sign of the goodness of heaven. R/ You, who in stall the saints manifest your face and presence, — grant, that your faithful, their their intercession, might always life with you. R/ You, who are goodness and mercy, accept our departed brothers to the eternal communion with your saints in heaven, — and grant to us, that we might be united eternally with you in your glory. R/

Our Father…

Almighty ever-living God, who ever adorn, protect and keep your church with the different splendours of the saints, graciously grant that by the prayers of blessed Paul the First Hermit and his holy sons and daughters, the merits of all of whom you have granted us to honour on one feast day, both that we may be purified from every fault and that we may fully enjoy eternal glory in the future. who lives and reigns with You in the unity of the Holy Spirit, one God for ever and ever.
